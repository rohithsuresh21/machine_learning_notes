IMP NOTES ON ML

Since you are just starting ML, here are three things to notice in the code:

train_test_split: We never show the model the "Test" data during training. We use it at the end to see if the model actually learned or just memorized.
X vs y: By convention, X (uppercase) is a matrix of inputs, and y (lowercase) is a vector of outputs.
Linearity: Linear Regression assumes that if you add 10 runs to the current score, the final score will likely increase by a fixed amount.


ðŸŸ¢ Pandas (The Data Manager)
Pandas is for cleaning and exploring your tables (DataFrames).

pd.read_csv(): The "Open" button. It loads your data from a file into a Python table.

df.head(): The "Quick Peek." Shows the first 5 rows so you can see if the data loaded correctly.

df.info(): The "Health Check." Tells you which columns have missing values (NaN) and what type of data (numbers vs. words) they hold.

df.describe(): The "Stat Summary." Instantly gives you the mean, min, max, and standard deviation of all your number columns.

df.iloc[] / df.loc[]: The "Scalpel." Used to cut out specific rows or columns based on position (iloc) or name (loc).

df.fillna(): The "Repair Kit." Fills in missing data with a specific value (like 0 or the average).

df.groupby(): The "Segmenter." Groups data by a category (e.g., group by 'Team' to see the average score per team).

pd.get_dummies(): The "Translator." Turns text categories (CSK, MI) into 1s and 0s so the math model can understand them.

ðŸ”µ NumPy (The Math Engine)
NumPy handles the heavy lifting of numbers and arrays.

np.array(): The "Constructor." Converts a standard Python list into a high-performance NumPy array.

np.arange(): The "Sequencer." Creates an array of numbers in order (e.g., 1 to 20).

arr.reshape(): The "Transformer." Changes the shape of your data (e.g., turning a flat list of 12 numbers into a 3x4 grid) without changing the numbers.

np.mean() / np.median(): The "Averages." Quickly finds the center point of your data.

np.sum(): The "Totalizer." Adds up all numbers in an arrayâ€”much faster than a Python for loop.

np.random.rand(): The "Shuffler." Generates random numbers, which is vital for initializing models or shuffling data.

ðŸŸ¡ Scikit-Learn (The ML Brain)
This library provides the tools to actually build and test your "AI."

train_test_split(): The "Exam Prep." It splits your data into two piles: one to study (Train) and one for the final exam (Test).

model.fit(): The "Learning Phase." This is where the model looks at the training data and finds the patterns.

model.predict(): The "Action Phase." This is where you give the model new data, and it gives you its best guess.

model.score(): The "Report Card." Gives you a quick accuracy percentage on how well the model is performing.

StandardScaler(): The "Leveler." If one column has small numbers (0-1) and another has huge numbers (10,000+), this scales them so the model doesn't get confused.

mean_absolute_error(): The "Reality Check." Tells you exactly how many units (or runs, in your case) your predictions are off by on average.
